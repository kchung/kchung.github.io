One Year Ago

Jun 11, 2013

On this faithful day, one year ago, I became a full-time employee at Weebly. It was hands down one of the best decisions I've made in my life.

The journey started shortly after I graduated from college. I was young and obsessed with startups. I frequented Hacker News while I was working on some contracting work and I ran into a post about Y Combinator's Work at a Startup 2012. I applied since it wasn't too far away from Reno and was accepted.

At the time, I've never experienced a shred silicon valley startup culture. When I showed up to the sketch warehouse in Mountain View for the event, I was decked out in slacks, nice shoes, and a button up. Everyone else was wearing shorts, flip-flops, and the iconic plaid shirt. I was instantly a foreigner.

A bunch of YC companies talked about their startup, there were so many that it was hard to remember what each one did. "We haven't slept in three weeks..", "It all started on an air mattress..", "We have a secret room..", and "Profitable.." are just a few of the quotes I still remember hearing that night.

After the presentations, we were fed burgers and beer. I waddled around and talked to a few founders that I circled in my little notebook. Towards the end of the night and after a few beers, I have to say I felt preetty good.

In my pocket were a bunch of my hand-cut business cards that I thought were cool. They had my website, my contact information, my email address, and a blank face on them. The business card required me to hand-draw a different face for every card I gave out. This night I gave out some pirate faces, smilies with mustaches, and squinty eyes. I got a few good chuckles after giving them out so I thought it was neat.

I ran into David Rusenko, and we talked about our travels in Italy, his company, and my life. Conversation was good but he had to run. I juggled around my beer, a pen, and a card where I proceeded to bend over and draw him a cool guy with some shades B) on my thigh. I was thinking how cool this face was going to end up before realizing I was spilling beer all the place like a drunko.

He laughed it off and I left the event embarrassed. I spent the next day coding at a Starbucks and received an email to come check out the Weebly office from Dave.

I wear plaid to work now.
